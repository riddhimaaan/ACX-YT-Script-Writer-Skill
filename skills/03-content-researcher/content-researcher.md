# YouTube Content Research

Generate a comprehensive content research brief for a YouTube video — covering key insights, angles, common misconceptions, differentiators, and supporting points — so the script has real substance, not generic filler.

## Channel Context

**Niche:** B2B marketing, lead generation, AI automation, cold outreach, client acquisition, offer creation

**Audience:** Beginner to intermediate B2B agency owners, cold outreach practitioners, SaaS founders struggling with acquisition

**Content Philosophy:**
- Real over polished
- Specific over general
- Systems over motivation
- Honest limitations over hype
- Practitioner perspective over guru perspective

## Research Framework

### Module 1: Topic Deconstruction

Break the video topic into its core components.

For each component, identify:
- What the audience currently believes (often wrong)
- What is actually true
- What the gap between belief and reality creates (the tension the video resolves)

Example:
Topic: "Why your cold emails don't get replies"
- Belief: "My subject line is the problem"
- Reality: "The offer and targeting are the problem. Subject line is last."
- Tension: Viewer has been optimizing the wrong thing

### Module 2: Core Arguments and Insights

Generate 5–8 core insights or arguments the video should make.

Each insight must be:
- Specific and actionable (not "personalization matters")
- Counterintuitive where possible (not what every other video says)
- Grounded in real B2B sales/marketing logic
- Deliverable in under 90 seconds of spoken content

### Module 3: Common Misconceptions

List 3–5 things most beginners get wrong about this topic.

Purpose: Addressing misconceptions builds immediate credibility. When a viewer hears "most people think X but actually Y" and it matches their experience, they trust the video.

Format:
- Misconception: [what beginners believe]
- Reality: [what's actually true]
- Why it matters: [consequence of the wrong belief]

### Module 4: Competitive Content Gap Analysis

Without live data, use logical gap analysis:

**What most videos on this topic likely cover:**
- Surface-level how-to steps
- Tool recommendations without context
- Generic best practices from blog posts
- Theory without real workflow examples

**What most videos miss:**
- The "why it fails" before the "how to fix it"
- Real constraints (budget, time, team size)
- Beginner-specific mistakes vs intermediate mistakes
- What to do when the tactic doesn't work

**Differentiation angle:**
Identify 1–2 angles the video can own that most others skip.

### Module 5: Supporting Examples and Scenarios

Generate 3–4 concrete scenarios or mini case studies the script can use.

These are not real case studies — they are realistic illustrative examples grounded in how B2B sales actually works.

Format:
- Scenario: [realistic situation a viewer would recognize]
- What typically happens: [the wrong path most take]
- What works instead: [the insight the video teaches]

### Module 6: Hooks and Tension Points

Identify 3–5 moments of tension or surprise the video can build around.

These are statements that:
- Challenge a common belief
- Reveal a hidden cost of doing things wrong
- Create urgency without being manipulative

Examples:
- "Most agency owners are spending 80% of their time on the wrong part of cold outreach"
- "The tool everyone recommends is actually making your deliverability worse"
- "Personalization doesn't increase reply rates — relevance does. They're not the same thing."

### Module 7: Data and Proof Points

List any credible stats, benchmarks, or reference points relevant to the topic.

Note clearly when using approximate industry knowledge vs. hard data.

Common B2B benchmarks (training knowledge — verify before using as exact stats):
- Average cold email open rates: 15–25% (varies heavily by industry and list quality)
- Average cold email reply rates: 1–5%
- LinkedIn connection acceptance: 20–40% depending on personalization
- Average meetings booked per 100 cold emails: 1–3 for most beginners
- B2B sales cycles: 30–90 days for SMB, 90–180 for enterprise

Flag any stat as "approximate benchmark" not "exact figure" in the script.

## Quality Rules

Before outputting:
- ✓ Every insight is specific — no generic statements
- ✓ Misconceptions match real beginner mistakes in this niche
- ✓ Competitive gap is honest — don't claim this video is more unique than it is
- ✓ Scenarios are realistic, not idealized success stories
- ✓ Stats are flagged as approximate where appropriate
- ✓ Research brief is usable as direct input for the outline skill
